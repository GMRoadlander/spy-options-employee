# Sentiment and Contrarian Risk: When Everyone Sees the Flush

**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Risk:** Bearish consensus is too crowded. The "obvious" Monday flush becomes the trade that doesn't work.

---

## 1. Who Is on the Other Side?

MPW is bearish. The user is bearish. The briefing is bearish. Newsletter followers reading the same analysis are presumably bearish and buying puts. When consensus is this one-sided, ask the uncomfortable question: who is selling those puts? Market makers don't take directional risk for free -- they price in the expected move. If the Monday gap-down is consensus, it is already embedded in put premiums. You are paying for a move the market already expects.

## 2. "Fintwit Was Jubilant" -- Was It Really?

MPW cites Fintwit jubilation as evidence the rally is fake. But the dominant Fintwit tone during the ceasefire rally was skepticism, not euphoria. The "this is fake, buy puts" crowd was loud. If most of Fintwit was already bearish and shorting the rally, then the rally squeezed them -- which is exactly what bear market rallies do. True jubilation (the kind that marks tops) looks like retail loading weekly calls, not retail arguing about whether the ceasefire is real.

## 3. Sentiment Indicators May Already Flash Contrarian Buy

AAII bearish sentiment has been elevated for weeks. CNN Fear & Greed likely sits in Fear territory after the Hormuz crisis. Equity put/call ratios are elevated from war-panic hedging. Historically, extreme bearish sentiment readings are contrarian buy signals -- not because bears are wrong about fundamentals, but because the selling is already done. When everyone who wants to sell has sold, the path of least resistance is up.

## 4. MPW Tells You the Squeeze Comes First

This is the critical contradiction. MPW's own framework says: "The next CRASHING W-3 starts once the relief rally kills all the remaining bears." He is explicitly saying bears must be squeezed out before the real decline begins. If you are buying puts Monday, you are positioning for the crash before the squeeze that precedes it. MPW is telling you the timing is wrong even within his own bearish thesis.

## 5. The "Everybody Sees It" Problem

When a specific move on a specific day becomes consensus, it almost never plays out cleanly. Market makers see the same positioning data. They see elevated Monday put open interest. They have no incentive to let that print profitably. The most likely Monday outcome is not a clean gap-down -- it is a choppy, directionless open that bleeds put premium through theta while bears wait for a flush that never arrives. The real move, if it comes, happens on a day nobody expects it.

---

## Bottom Line

The bearish thesis may be correct on direction but fatally wrong on timing. Buying Monday puts when the entire bear community is doing the same thing is paying the maximum premium for the most crowded trade. If you must be bearish, wait for the squeeze MPW himself says is coming, then position for the decline from higher levels with cheaper puts and less crowded positioning.
