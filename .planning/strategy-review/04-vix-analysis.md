# Adversarial Review: "VIX at 20 = Puts Are Cheap"

**Claim:** VIX collapsed 5.6 pts to ~20 after the ceasefire, making puts cheap for Thursday entry.

**Verdict:** Puts are cheaper than Tuesday. They are not cheap.

---

## 1. VIX 20 Is Still Elevated

Pre-war VIX was 12-15. Long-run median is ~17. VIX 20 means annualized vol of 20% -- above-average, not a discount. The briefing anchors "cheap" to the war spike instead of baseline. Current puts are 33-60% pricier in vol terms than the pre-conflict regime.

## 2. VIX Measures 30-Day Vol -- Weeklies Live Elsewhere

VIX derives from 30-day SPX options. The strategies use 0-5 DTE weeklies on a different part of the term structure. After a vol crush, 30-day vol drops while sub-7-day IV stays sticky because near-term catalysts (PCE Thursday, CPI Friday) are unresolved. Weekly IV could be 22-25 while VIX reads 20 -- a term-structure premium. Without checking actual weekly IV, citing VIX as a proxy is a category error.

## 3. Put Skew Already Prices the Monday Flush

If "everyone expects a gap down Monday," 25-delta put skew is already bid. The trade is only cheap if the expected move exceeds what skew implies. The briefing never compares expected vs. priced-in move. Without that, "puts are cheap" is a feeling, not analysis.

## 4. VIX Could Compress Further Into Friday

A 5.6-point single-day crush signals aggressive vol selling. If the ceasefire holds and PCE prints benign, VIX could drift to 17-18 by Friday. Thursday entry buys puts 2-3 vol points above Friday's potential close. Entering before PCE means owning puts through a vol-crush catalyst -- if tame, puts bleed theta and vega simultaneously.

## 5. IV Rank/Percentile Context Is Missing

VIX 20 in isolation means nothing. If the 52-week range was 11-26, VIX 20 is the 60th percentile -- not cheap. No IV rank, no percentile, no comparison to realized vol. If realized vol is 18, the volatility risk premium is only 2 pts -- thin margin for a put-buying thesis.

---

**Bottom line:** The briefing conflates a relative price drop with an absolute bargain, ignores the weekly/monthly term structure mismatch, and never checks whether skew already prices the bearish consensus. A rigorous entry needs actual weekly IV, skew levels, and IV rank -- not a VIX headline.
