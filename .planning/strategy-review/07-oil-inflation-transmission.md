# Adversarial Review: Oil Spike -> Hot CPI/PCE Assumption

**Claim**: Weeks of $100+ oil from the Hormuz blockade guarantee a hot PCE/CPI print, justifying bearish positioning ahead of Thursday's release.

**Verdict**: The timing is wrong, the metric is wrong, and the market may already be pricing the opposite.

---

## 1. Transmission Lag -- FATAL TIMING PROBLEM

Oil price shocks do not appear in inflation data instantly. There are two channels:

- **Direct (gasoline, heating oil)**: Retail pump prices follow crude with a 2-4 week lag. The BLS and BEA sample prices mid-month. A March oil spike hits March data at earliest, April data more reliably.
- **Indirect (freight, packaging, airfare)**: Transport cost pass-through takes 2-3 months. Producers absorb short spikes rather than reprice immediately.

Thursday's PCE report covers **February**. The Hormuz crisis started in March. February crude averaged roughly $78-82/bbl -- pre-crisis levels. The oil spike is structurally incapable of appearing in this print. You are trading a March shock against February data.

## 2. Core PCE Strips Energy -- THE MARKET WATCHES CORE

Headline PCE includes energy. Core PCE excludes food and energy. The Fed's preferred gauge -- and the number that moves markets -- is core. Even if gasoline somehow spiked in February (it didn't), core PCE would be unaffected by construction. The briefing conflates "oil is expensive" with "inflation is hot" without specifying which metric. For the number that actually matters to rate expectations, oil is irrelevant.

## 3. Oil Already Crashed -- FORWARD vs. BACKWARD

Crude hit ~$112 at the Hormuz peak and has since collapsed 16% to ~$94. Markets are forward-looking. By Thursday, traders will be pricing the deflationary signal from the crash, not the inflationary signal from the spike. A backward-looking hot print (if one even materializes in later months) arriving alongside collapsing oil prices would likely be dismissed as stale -- the classic "looking in the rearview mirror" reaction.

## 4. Spike Duration Matters -- SHORT SPIKES WASH OUT

Historical precedent: the 2019 Aramco attack spiked oil 15% intraday; prices normalized within two weeks and left zero imprint on CPI. Sustained multi-quarter shocks (2021-2022) do flow through. A spike lasting weeks, now reversing, falls in the "wash out" category. Producers do not reprice supply chains for a transient disruption.

## 5. Base Effects Work Against the Thesis

Year-over-year comparisons for February would reference February 2025, when oil was also in the mid-$70s range. There is no favorable base effect that would mechanically push the YoY number higher from oil alone.

## Bottom Line

The oil-to-inflation thesis has four independent failure points: wrong data month, wrong inflation metric, wrong price direction, and wrong duration. Any one of these breaks the trade. All four together make it a house built on sand. If Thursday's PCE comes in hot, it will be because of shelter, insurance, or services -- not oil. Position accordingly.
