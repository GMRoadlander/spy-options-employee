# Borey's Grade: Strategy Briefing Review

**Date:** 2026-04-06

---

## 1. THESIS -- "Delayed Monday Flush" | Grade: C+

The directional read is not wrong. Two-week conditional ceasefires with no underlying resolution map closest to Ukraine 2022 -- those rallies reversed. But the briefing treats Monday as a certainty when the base rate for large Monday geopolitical gaps is under 10%. The PCE/CPI angle is structurally broken: February PCE has zero oil pass-through, March CPI catches only the tail. The "shrug then crack" sequence requires four independent links to hold simultaneously. Thesis direction is defensible. Thesis timing is a guess dressed up as analysis.

## 2. STRATEGY SELECTION | Grade: D+

Three strategies, three problems. The put debit spread anchors strikes to round numbers with no GEX or volume profile justification. The ATM straddle at Friday 3:30 PM is when professionals sell straddles, not buy them -- weekend theta eats 30-40% of premium before Monday opens. The short call spread at Fibonacci 6800 has 5:1 adverse risk/reward on a strike built on empirically debunked methodology. None of the three structures match the thesis they are supposed to express. If you believe Monday flushes, you enter Friday close with a put spread anchored to dealer positioning -- not Thursday morning paying double event premium for data that is not even your catalyst.

## 3. RISK MANAGEMENT | Grade: F

This is what fails the briefing. No position sizing as a percentage of account. No max loss in dollars. No exit plan if wrong. No exit plan if right. No plan for "thesis correct, timing off by one day." No stop-loss levels. No acknowledgment that bearish consensus is crowded and put skew already prices the Monday gap. The briefing presents three trades with zero risk framework around any of them. Direction without risk management is gambling.

## 4. PRESENTATION | Grade: B-

The research quality is genuinely above average for a new trader. Fibonacci analysis, Elliott Wave, geopolitical analogs, VIX context -- this is more homework than most people do in a year. The adversarial self-review is impressive. But an experienced trader reads past the research and looks for the five numbers: size, max loss, entry trigger, stop, target. None are present. Beautiful thesis, no execution plan.

The put spread is the only defensible vehicle, but the strikes are lazy and the entry timing is two days early.

You have a paper trading system. Use it. Run this thesis on paper four times before you risk a dollar. And when you come back, lead with your position size and stop-loss, not your Elliott Wave count."

## 6. What Borey Would Actually Trade

Wait. Not Monday -- that is the crowded trade. Wait for the squeeze MPW himself says comes first. If the ceasefire quietly extends through the week and SPX grinds to 6850-6900, bears get squeezed out, put premiums collapse, and VIX drops to 16-17. THAT is when you buy puts -- when nobody wants them anymore, when they are actually cheap, not just cheaper than the war spike. A 6800/6700 put spread two weeks out, entered after the squeeze, sized at 1% of account with a 50% stop. Patient, boring, and profitable.

**Overall: C | Shows promise, not ready for live capital on this setup.**
