# Adversarial Review: Position Sizing and Risk Management for a New Options Trader

**Claim:** Enter SPX/SPY options Thursday morning based on a Monday flush thesis.

**Verdict:** The trade idea is secondary. What Borey will evaluate is the risk management plan around it. A new trader without position sizing discipline will blow up regardless of whether the thesis is correct.

---

## 1. Position Sizing: The Only Rule That Matters

Industry standard for directional options: risk 1-2% of total account per trade. If your account is $10,000, your maximum loss on this trade is $100-$200. Not your position size -- your maximum loss. A new trader should start at 1%. MPW caps his speculative account at $100K and still sizes individual trades small relative to that. You are not MPW. Start smaller than you think is reasonable, then cut it in half again.

## 2. Weeklies Will Punish Timing Errors Brutally

Thursday-to-Monday weeklies give you one day of float before weekend theta eats your premium alive. If your Monday flush arrives Tuesday instead, you can lose 50-70% of premium waiting one extra day. New traders consistently underestimate how fast weekly options decay. A thesis that is directionally correct but wrong by 24 hours is still a losing trade. If you must use weeklies, accept that timing precision is the trade -- not direction.

## 3. Defined Risk or Nothing

Every strategy must have a known maximum loss before entry. No naked short options, ever. If you are buying puts or calls outright, the max loss is the premium paid -- acceptable, as long as that premium respects the 1-2% rule. If you are constructing spreads, the max loss is the width of the spread minus credit received. Write the number down before you enter. If you cannot state your max loss in dollars, you do not have a trade -- you have a gamble.

## 4. The Conviction Trap

"I think the market flushes Monday" is a thesis, not an edge. New traders overtrade conviction because it feels like certainty. What if you are wrong? Do you add to the position? Do you hold through expiry hoping? Do you double down Thursday afternoon? The plan for being wrong must exist before the trade is placed. If the plan is "I'll figure it out," you will freeze, hold, and lose more than you should have.

## 5. Paper Trade This First

You have a paper trading system. Use it. Run this exact thesis -- Thursday entry, Monday flush target -- on paper for 4-6 weeks. Track win rate, average gain, average loss, and max drawdown. If the strategy cannot make money on paper with no slippage and no emotions, it will not make money with real capital and real fear. Paper trading is not optional for a new trader. It is the minimum.

## 6. What Borey Will Actually Want to See

Borey does not need another trade idea. He needs to see: (a) position size as a percentage of account, (b) exact max loss in dollars, (c) exit plan if wrong -- at what loss do you cut, (d) exit plan if right -- at what gain do you take profit, (e) what happens if the thesis is right but timing is off by a day. Present those five numbers and Borey will take you seriously. Present only the thesis and he will tell you to come back when you have a plan.
