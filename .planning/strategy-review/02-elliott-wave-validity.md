# Adversarial Review: MPW Elliott Wave Analysis

## 1. Wave Count Ambiguity

Elliott Wave theory requires subjective pattern recognition. Two credentialed EW practitioners routinely produce contradictory counts on identical charts. MPW's Wave-2 count is invalidated if price exceeds the Wave-1 origin (7002). That is a hard EW rule -- if SPX prints above 7002, the entire "generational bear" count is dead. Additionally, Wave-2 cannot retrace more than 100% of Wave-1. The current rally to 6783 from a presumed Wave-1 low leaves uncomfortably little room before invalidation. Any count that lives this close to its own kill zone deserves deep skepticism.

## 2. The "Generational Bear" Catalyst Problem

Sub-3000 SPX implies a 57%+ decline -- comparable to 2008-2009. Every generational bear had an identifiable structural catalyst: dot-com (2000) had a decade of speculative excess unwinding plus accounting fraud; the GFC (2008) had systemic leverage through toxic mortgage instruments threatening the entire banking system. What is the equivalent here? Tariff escalation is real but already showing de-escalation signals. The US economy is not running on fraudulent balance sheets. Corporate earnings remain broadly intact. A 57% decline without a credit crisis, a banking collapse, or a systemic liquidity event has no modern precedent. "Tariffs cause recession" does not map to "worse than 2008." The burden of proof for a generational call is on the claimant, and it is not met.

## 3. The 60/40 Probability Problem

A 60% bear / 40% bull split between outcomes of "crash to 3000" and "rally to 7100" is analytically useless for position sizing. These are not adjacent outcomes -- they are maximally divergent. If your framework cannot distinguish between an apocalyptic crash and a bullish breakout with better than 60/40 confidence, it is telling you it does not know. Presenting this as actionable guidance is irresponsible. A coin flip with narrative dressing is still a coin flip.

## 4. EW Track Record

No peer-reviewed study has validated Elliott Wave as a predictive framework with statistical significance. Practitioners publicize wins and silently revise misses by updating the "alternate count." This creates survivorship bias in any self-reported accuracy claim. The framework is unfalsifiable in practice: when the count fails, it was the "alternate" all along. Academic literature (e.g., Poser 2003, Batchelor & Ramyar 2006) finds no evidence that EW patterns predict market direction better than chance.

## 5. Over-Reliance in Strategy Briefing

Anchoring a strategy briefing to one technician's subjective wave count is a structural error. MPW's levels (exhaustion zone, H&S neckline, W-2 top) are presented as if they are empirical facts rather than one person's interpretation. For an experienced trader like Borey, the briefing should present MPW as one input among many -- weighted no higher than vol surface data, macro positioning, or flow analysis. Currently, MPW's framing dominates the narrative. That is a single point of failure in the analytical process.

## Verdict

The "generational bear" call is an extraordinary claim lacking extraordinary evidence. Use MPW's levels as reference points, not as the scaffolding for an entire trading thesis.
