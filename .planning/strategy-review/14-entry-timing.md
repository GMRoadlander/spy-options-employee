# Adversarial Review: Options Entry Timing Around PCE/CPI

**Claim:** Enter Thursday morning for a Monday flush thesis. SPX ~6783, VIX ~20.

**Verdict:** Thursday morning is the worst of all available entries. Friday close dominates on every axis.

---

## 1. Pre-PCE Thursday Entry Is Paying Peak IV for the Privilege of Being Early

IV term structure into known macro events follows a predictable pattern: implied vol ramps into the event, peaks in the hours before release, then crushes within 30-60 minutes post-print. Entering before 8:30 AM Thursday means buying options at the local IV maximum. You are paying the event premium for PCE, then paying it again for CPI Friday. A Thursday morning put buyer is buying two event premiums they do not need -- neither event is the catalyst in the thesis. The catalyst is supposed to be Monday. You are subsidizing market makers' weekend in the Hamptons.

## 2. Post-PCE Thursday Is Better but Still Suboptimal

After PCE drops and IV crushes, premiums cheapen. But you still carry Thursday afternoon + all of Friday as dead theta. If the thesis is "market holds through Friday, flushes Monday," then every hour of Thursday and Friday is pure time decay with no expected payoff. At VIX 20, a near-ATM SPX weekly put loses roughly $15-25 per contract per day in theta. Two days of carry on a position that is not supposed to move is burning $30-50 per contract before the thesis even activates.

## 3. Friday Close Is the Structurally Correct Entry

If the thesis is a Monday gap-down, the optimal entry is Friday between 3:00-3:45 PM ET, after both data events have resolved. At that point: (a) both event premiums have crushed out of IV, (b) weekend theta has partially decayed the Friday premium, (c) you carry only weekend risk -- which is the actual thesis, and (d) you avoid two full days of theta burn on a position that needs the weekend to work. The only argument against Friday close is if you believe PCE/CPI themselves cause the Monday move -- but that contradicts the "shrug it off, flush Monday" thesis. Pick one narrative.

## 4. The Exception: If You Think PCE/CPI Are the Trigger

If the real thesis is "hot data Thursday/Friday causes a selloff that extends into Monday," then the entry shifts to post-data confirmation. Wait for the print, see if the market actually sells, and enter on the continuation. Entering before data is a coin flip with overpaid premium. But note: review 05 already established that the data likely does not print hot enough to surprise consensus.

---

**Bottom line:** Thursday morning is the maximum-cost, maximum-theta-burn entry point for a thesis that requires Monday to work. Friday close after both events have cleared is cheaper, cleaner, and aligned with the actual catalyst window. The only reason to enter Thursday is if you do not actually believe your own thesis and want exposure to the data events themselves -- in which case, say that.
