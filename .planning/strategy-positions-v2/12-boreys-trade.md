## Why These Exact Strikes

I'm not being arbitrary here. Every strike has a reason.

**$674 long put.** SPY is at $678. The $674 put is $4 out of the money, delta around 0.35-0.38. I'm not buying the ATM $678 put because I'd be paying for the first $4 of decline that the market gives up for free in the first 30 minutes of most mornings. And I'm not going deep OTM at $668 because then I need SPY to fall $10 before I start making money, and theta eats me alive waiting. The $674 is the sweet spot: close enough that a real move makes it print, far enough OTM that I'm not overpaying.

**$664 short put.** The gap fill target is SPY ~$661. My short leg is $3 above that. If SPY fills the gap to $661, my spread is fully in the money. I don't need SPY to crash through the gap -- I just need the gap to fill. The $3 cushion above the target means I capture the full payout even if the gap fill undershoots slightly. And the short leg sells back some of the inflated IV I'm paying on the long side. At VIX 20, that matters.

**$10 width.** I want a wider spread here, not a $5. A $5 spread (674/669) would cost about $1.80 and max at $3.20. That's 1.8:1 -- not compelling enough for the commission and attention. The $10 width costs $3.00 and maxes at $7.00. That's 2.3:1. The extra $1.20 I'm paying buys $3.80 of additional profit potential. I'll take that math every time.

**Apr 11 expiry (Friday weekly).** This is deliberate. I want the position to resolve THIS WEEK. The gap fill either happens on the PCE/CPI catalysts or it doesn't. I'm not paying for extra time I don't need. The Apr 17 expiry would cost $0.80-1.00 more per spread for 6 extra days I don't want. If my thesis is right, the move happens by Friday. If I'm wrong, I want to be out and move on -- not sitting in a losing position for another week hoping something changes.

## Why Not Other Structures

Every other trade I've ever done that went wrong, I can trace back to complexity I didn't need. Let me save you the tuition:

**Naked puts.** VIX is 20. A 4DTE $674 put costs about $4.50. Theta burns $0.80-$1.00 per day. If SPY chops sideways Monday-Wednesday and then fills the gap Thursday-Friday, the put has lost $2.40 to theta before the move even starts. My spread loses maybe $0.45 in that same period. The spread survives the wait.

**Butterflies.** Beautiful on a spreadsheet. Need SPY to pin a specific level at expiration. SPY doesn't pin levels -- it blows through them or reverses. A butterfly centered at $665 pays 8:1 if SPY lands exactly at $665 but pays almost nothing at $660 or $670. I'm not that precise. Nobody is.

**Iron condors.** A bet that SPY stays in a range. I don't think SPY stays in a range. I think it fills a gap. Selling premium against my own thesis doesn't make sense.

**Calendar spreads.** If the gap fills fast (which is what I want), the calendar loses money because both legs go deep ITM and the extrinsic differential collapses. A trade that loses when my thesis works perfectly is a bad trade.

**Adding a call hedge.** Some people would put on a call spread as a hedge in case the ceasefire holds. I don't hedge a defined-risk trade. My max loss is $900 (realistically $300). That IS my hedge. The defined risk IS the protection. Hedging the hedge is paying double for insurance.
