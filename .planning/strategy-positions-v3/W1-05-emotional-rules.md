EMOTIONAL MANAGEMENT RULES -- V3 Swarm
=======================================
Date: 2026-04-06 (Sunday)
Account: $10,000
Max combined risk: $500 (5% of account)
Context: Gil's FIRST real options trades. Strong conviction (Iran intel,
         MPW exhaustion zone, Borey's gap fill call). This is exactly when
         emotional rules matter most.

Warning: Strong conviction + new trader = the most dangerous combination
in trading. These rules exist BECAUSE you believe in the thesis. They do
not exist for trades you are unsure about. Those trades you simply skip.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

BEFORE ENTRY
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Rule 1: THE PRE-MORTEM

Before placing ANY order, write down -- on paper, not in your head:

    "If I lose $[max loss], I will feel _____________ and I will want
     to do _____________."

Fill in both blanks honestly. There are three answers that disqualify
the trade:

    DISQUALIFYING ANSWER #1: "I will want to add more contracts."
    DISQUALIFYING ANSWER #2: "I will want to immediately enter a new trade."
    DISQUALIFYING ANSWER #3: "I cannot accept this loss."

If any of those is your honest answer, you are oversized. Cut the position
in half and redo the exercise. If it still triggers a disqualifying answer,
you are not ready to trade this thesis today. Walk away. The market will
be here tomorrow.

The CORRECT answer looks like: "I will feel frustrated, and I will close
the app and review the trade journal tomorrow."

Rule 2: ALL EXITS SET BEFORE ENTRY

Before you click "Submit Order" on ANY position, the following must
already be set as live orders or hard alerts in your broker:

    [ ] Stop loss order or alert (exact price, written down)
    [ ] Profit target order or alert (exact price, written down)
    [ ] Time stop (calendar reminder with alarm -- date AND time)
    [ ] Thesis invalidation level (SPY price where the thesis is DEAD)

If you cannot define all four, you do not understand the trade well
enough to enter it. This is not optional. This is not "I will set them
after I get filled." Fills happen in seconds. Discipline erodes in
minutes. Set the exits FIRST, then enter.

Rule 3: THE 90-SECOND RULE

After placing each order, close the broker app for 90 seconds. If the
order fills, your alerts are already set (Rule 2). If it does not fill,
you check once and adjust the limit price per the entry plan. Then close
again.

This breaks the dopamine loop of watching the order book tick. You are
not a market maker. You do not need to see every tick.

-----------------------------------------------------------------

DURING THE TRADE
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Rule 4: THREE CHECK-INS PER DAY, MAXIMUM

You may check your positions at these times and ONLY these times:

    10:00 AM ET -- Morning check after opening volatility settles
    1:00 PM ET  -- Midday check (lunch break, quick glance)
    3:30 PM ET  -- End-of-day check before close

Between those times, CLOSE THE BROKER APP. Close the SPY chart. Close
Twitter/X. Close Reddit. Close the Discord trading channels. If your
phone has a screen time feature, block the broker app between check-ins.

Exception: If a hard alert fires (stop loss, profit target, or thesis
invalidation), you open the app, execute the predetermined action, and
close it again. An alert is not an invitation to "reassess." It is a
signal to execute the plan you already made.

Rule 5: THE "DOWN 20%" PROTOCOL

Your position is down 20% from entry (e.g., a $220 spread is now
worth $176). This is NORMAL. This is not a signal that the trade is
wrong. Do the following:

    1. Check the three thesis tests (Rule 10 below).
    2. If all three say "thesis alive" -- do nothing. Close the app.
    3. If any thesis test says "thesis dead" -- close the position NOW.
    4. Do NOT move your stop loss. The stop was set before entry for a
       reason. That reason has not changed because your P&L turned red.

What you will WANT to do: check Twitter for confirmation bias, text
Borey asking "is the trade still good?", calculate what happens if you
average down. Do NONE of these things.

Rule 6: THE "DOWN 40%" PROTOCOL

Your position is down 40% (e.g., a $220 spread is now worth $132).
This hurts. Your body is telling you to do something -- anything -- to
make the pain stop. Here is what you do:

    1. Check if your hard stop has been hit. If yes, the position is
       already closing. Let it execute. Walk away.
    2. If the stop has NOT been hit (because you set it wider than 40%),
       run the thesis tests (Rule 10).
    3. If thesis alive: hold. You sized for this. A $220 spread going
       to zero loses $220 -- that is 2.2% of your account. You survive.
    4. If thesis dead: close immediately. Do not "give it one more day."

What you will WANT to do: move the stop loss further away to "give the
trade room." This is the single most destructive behavior in trading.
A stop that moves is not a stop. It is a wish.

Rule 7: THE "UP 50%" PROTOCOL

Your position is up 50% (e.g., a $220 spread is now worth $330,
meaning about $110 unrealized profit). The thesis is working. Do this:

    1. Check your profit target. Is the position at or above it? If
       yes, CLOSE. Take the money. Full stop.
    2. If below your profit target, set a TRAILING FLOOR at breakeven.
       Meaning: if the position drops back to $220 (your entry cost),
       you close for scratch. You will not let a winner become a loser.
    3. Do NOT add to the position. "It is working, I should add more"
       is how new traders turn a $110 gain into a $400 loss on a reversal.

Rule 8: THE "UP 100%" PROTOCOL (or any amount that feels life-changing)

Your position has doubled. The gap fill is happening. You feel euphoric.
This is the SECOND most dangerous moment (the first is a big loss).

    1. Close at least HALF the position. If you hold 1 contract, that
       means close the entire thing. On a $10K account with 1-contract
       positions, you do not have the luxury of scaling out. TAKE THE
       MONEY.
    2. If you are in a spread that is near max profit (e.g., a $10-wide
       put spread worth $8.50), close it. You are risking $8.50 of
       unrealized gain to capture $1.50 more. That is terrible math.
    3. Write down: "I just closed a trade at 100% profit. This is what
       good trading feels like." Then CLOSE THE APP. Do not immediately
       enter another trade on the rush.

The urge will be: "If I am this right about the gap fill, I should
enter another position to capture the rest of the move." No. You
planned three positions before the week started. You do not add a
fourth because the first one worked. That is how a $1,000 gain week
becomes a -$300 week.

WHEN THE THESIS IS TESTED
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Rule 9: THE THREE THESIS TESTS

Before panicking about any position, answer these three questions:

    TEST 1: Is SPY above $685?
        YES = Thesis is weakening. Ceasefire rally extended beyond
              the exhaustion zone. Check your stop -- it should be
              close to triggering. Prepare to exit.
        NO  = Thesis is alive. Price is still in the range where
              the gap fill can start.

    TEST 2: Has VIX dropped below 16?
        YES = Market is complacent. The ceasefire is being priced
              as permanent. Your bearish thesis is losing its
              catalyst. Tighten stops.
        NO  = Market still sees risk. Your thesis has fuel.

    TEST 3: Is it past your time stop?
        YES = Close the position regardless of P&L. Time is up.
              The thesis may still be right, but your TRADE is
              over. There is a difference.
        NO  = You have time. Patience is not the same as hope.
              If the thesis is alive and time remains, the trade
              is working as designed.

If all three tests say "thesis alive" -- hold and follow the plan.
If any test says "thesis dead or expired" -- exit NOW, not later.

Rule 10: PCE COMES HOT AND THE MARKET RALLIES (Thursday April 9)

This is the scenario that will test your conviction hardest. PCE shows
inflation sticky, but SPY rallies because "rate cuts delayed = ceasefire
more important = rally continues." Your puts are red. The bear call is
under pressure. You feel sick.

    WHAT TO DO:
    1. Remember: PCE is February data. PRE-Hormuz. The market knows this.
       A rally on stale data is not a trend change.
    2. Run the three thesis tests (Rule 9). Is SPY above $685? No? Then
       the thesis is alive.
    3. Check your stops. If none triggered, the trade is within expected
       parameters. You sized for this.
    4. DO NOT add to your bearish positions "because the market is wrong."
       The market is not wrong. It is just not on your timeline.
    5. DO NOT flip to calls. Switching sides on the same day is revenge
       trading with extra steps.
    6. If your stops DO trigger: execute them. The plan worked as designed.
       A stop is not a failure. It is the system saving you from a bigger loss.

Rule 11: CEASEFIRE HEADLINE -- "Progress in Islamabad Talks" (Saturday April 11)

A weekend headline says talks are going well. Oil drops. Futures
indicate a gap-up Monday. Your weekend is ruined.

    WHAT TO DO:
    1. DO NOT log into your broker on Sunday night to "check damage."
       Pre-market on Sunday night is thin, illiquid, and not representative
       of Monday's actual open. Checking it will cause panic for no reason.
    2. Monday at 10:00 AM (your first check-in): run the thesis tests.
       SPY above $685? VIX below 16? If yes to both -- the thesis may be
       dead. Close positions at your stops.
    3. Remember the market context: Iran has 31 armed services. No single
       authority can enforce the ceasefire. "Progress in talks" and
       "enforceable ceasefire" are two different things. You know this
       better than the headline writers.
    4. IF you decide the thesis is dead: close everything calmly at market
       prices during the 10:00 AM check-in. Do not leave orders overnight
       on Sunday based on futures. Do not try to "get out early" at 9:31 AM
       in the opening chaos.
    5. After closing: DONE FOR THE DAY. Do not enter new positions.

Rule 12: SPY GAPS DOWN $5+ MONDAY (SPY opens at $673 or lower)

The thesis is working. The ceasefire is cracking. You feel vindicated.
This is dangerous.

    WHAT TO DO:
    1. DO NOT add to your positions. The gap down already happened. You
       are not buying puts after a $5 drop -- that is chasing.
    2. Check your profit targets. Any position at target? Close it.
    3. If the put spread is up 40-60% but not at target, set the trailing
       floor at breakeven (Rule 7). Let the thesis play out but protect
       the gain.
    4. If the butterfly (if you hold Position C from V2) is suddenly
       alive, set an alert at its profitable zone. Do not touch it yet --
       butterflies need more move to pay off.
    5. THE TEMPTATION: "The gap fill target is $661, SPY is at $673, I
       should add another put spread to capture the rest of the move."
       NO. You planned your positions on Sunday. Monday is for MANAGING,
       not ADDING.

Rule 13: SPY GAPS UP $5+ MONDAY (SPY opens at $683+)

The ceasefire is holding. The market is rallying. Your thesis is in
serious trouble.

    WHAT TO DO:
    1. At 10:00 AM check-in: run the thesis tests. SPY above $685? This
       is the danger zone.
    2. Check each position's stop. The bear call credit spread is now
       under pressure -- the short strike at $683-684 may be breached.
       If the stop triggers, let it execute.
    3. The long put / put spread is bleeding. Check: is it at your stop
       level? If yes, close. If no, the thesis gets until the time stop.
       A Monday gap up does not mean Friday will be higher.
    4. DO NOT "double down" on the bearish thesis by adding more puts.
       The market just told you something. Listen.
    5. IF all stops trigger and all positions close: you lost ~$360-480.
       That is 3.6-4.8% of your account. You have $9,520+ left. The
       account is fine. YOU are fine. Close the app. Review tomorrow.
    6. IS THE THESIS DEAD? Not necessarily. A gap up to $683 is still
       within the exhaustion zone (6775-6800 SPX). The thesis says this
       zone should hold. But if SPY closes above $686 on Monday, the
       exhaustion zone has failed and the bull path is in play. At that
       point, let time stops expire your remaining positions. Do not
       add new bearish trades.

AFTER THE TRADE (Win or Lose)
-----------------------------------------------------------------

Rule 14: THE TRADE JOURNAL (Mandatory, Every Trade, No Exceptions)

Within 1 hour of closing a position, write down (paper or notes app):

    Date: ___________
    Position: ___________
    Entry price: _______  Exit price: _______
    P&L: $_______  (____% of account)
    Holding period: _______ days

    What went right:
    1. ___________
    2. ___________

    What went wrong:
    1. ___________
    2. ___________

    Did I follow ALL my rules?
    - Honored stop loss?       YES / NO
    - Set exits before entry?  YES / NO
    - Checked only 3x/day?    YES / NO
    - Avoided adding size?     YES / NO
    - Closed at time stop?     YES / NO

    Grade: _____ (A through F, based on PROCESS, not P&L)

    What I will do differently next time:
    ___________________________________________

A losing trade where every answer is YES gets an A.
A winning trade where you broke rules gets an F.
File these. Review them before each new trade week.

Rule 15: THE 24-HOUR COOLING PERIOD

After ANY of these events, you do not trade for 24 hours:

    - A stop loss is hit (you lost money as planned -- still hurts)
    - You close all positions in a single day (win or lose)
    - You feel angry, frustrated, relieved, or euphoric about a result
    - You catch yourself thinking "I should have sized bigger"
    - You catch yourself thinking "I should have held longer"

During the 24-hour period, you may review your trade journal. You may
read market analysis. You may NOT open your broker, place orders, or
even look at your account balance.

This rule exists because revenge trading is the #1 account killer for
new traders. It does not feel like revenge trading when you are doing
it. It feels like "I have a new thesis" or "I see a better setup now."
But if the new thesis appeared within hours of a loss, it is not a
thesis. It is your ego trying to get even.

Rule 16: CELEBRATE PROCESS, NOT PROFIT

After this trading week, evaluate yourself on this scorecard:

    PROCESS SCORECARD (Week of April 7-11)
    ----------------------------------------
    Did I follow the entry plan timing?          __/10
    Did I honor every stop loss?                 __/10
    Did I limit check-ins to 3x/day?             __/10
    Did I avoid adding unplanned positions?       __/10
    Did I close at time stops?                   __/10
    Did I complete the trade journal each time?  __/10

    40+ = Excellent. You are building the habits that make traders
          survive their first year.
    25-39 = Acceptable. Identify which rule you broke most and

               habits are not ready for real capital.

A week where you lose $300 but score 55/60 is a GREAT WEEK. You paid
$300 in tuition and proved you can manage risk. That skill is worth
$100,000 over a trading career.

A week where you make $500 but score 20/60 is a TERRIBLE WEEK. You
got lucky. Luck runs out. Habits do not.

EMERGENCY PROTOCOL: "I AM ABOUT TO DO SOMETHING STUPID"
-----------------------------------------------------------------

You will know the moment. Your finger is hovering over "Buy" on an
unplanned trade. You are about to move your stop. You are about to
add a fourth position because "the thesis is so obvious."

When that moment comes:

    1. Close the laptop lid. Not minimize. CLOSE.
    2. Set a timer for 10 minutes.
    3. During those 10 minutes, answer ONE question out loud:
       "What does this trade look like if I lose the maximum amount?"
    4. If, after 10 minutes, you still want to do it, text Borey and
       describe the trade. If Borey says "do it," proceed. If Borey
       does not respond within 30 minutes, the answer is NO.
    5. If you cannot reach Borey, the answer is NO.

This is not a democracy. This is not a coin flip. The default answer
to any unplanned trade is NO. The burden of proof is on the trade to
justify itself to someone who is not emotionally invested in it.

WHY THESE RULES EXIST: THE MATH OF SURVIVAL
-----------------------------------------------------------------

At $10,000 with a 5% max weekly risk:

    - If you lose $500 this week, you have $9,500.
    - Next week at 5%, you risk $475. If you lose that, you have $9,025.
    - After 10 CONSECUTIVE losing weeks (which almost never happens),
      you still have $5,987.
    - You are still in the game. You can still learn. You can still win.

Without rules, here is what happens:

    - Week 1: Lose $500 (planned). Feel bad.
    - Week 2: "High conviction" -- risk $1,000. Lose $1,000. Now at $8,500.
    - Week 3: "I need to make it back" -- risk $1,500. Lose $800.
      Now at $7,700.
    - Week 4: "One big trade to get even" -- risk $2,000. Lose $2,000.
      Now at $5,700. Account down 43% in a month.
    - Week 5: You stop trading. The account sits at $5,700 for 6 months
      while you feel ashamed. Eventually you withdraw the money and
      tell yourself "options are rigged."

That is the path of EVERY new trader who does not have rules. Not some.
Every. The market does not care about your thesis. The market does not
care about your conviction. The market only cares about your survival,
because survivors get to trade tomorrow.

These rules are not training wheels. They are the seatbelt. You wear
the seatbelt every time you drive, not just when you feel unsafe. You
follow these rules every trade, not just when you feel uncertain.
