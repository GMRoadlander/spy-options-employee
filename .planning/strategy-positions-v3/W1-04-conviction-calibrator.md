## THE PRINCIPLE

Conviction changes SIZE, not STRUCTURE.

Whether you are 50% sure or 90% sure, you trade the same bear put spread. The same strikes. The same expiry. The same stop rules. What changes is the number of contracts and the dollars at risk. A high-conviction trader with bad structure still blows up. A low-conviction trader with good structure and proper sizing survives to trade again.

This is the lesson Borey hammered in his critique: every v2 position broke the 2% rule and rationalized it with "high conviction." The conviction calibrator exists to prevent that. You decide the conviction FIRST, read the sizing from the table, and execute. No negotiation. No "but this time is different."

## SIZING TABLE

The reference trade is a bear put spread with approximately $3.00 of width (max risk = $300 per contract at full debit). Actual spreads will vary -- adjust contract count proportionally if the spread width or debit differs. The dollar risk column is the HARD MAXIMUM for that conviction level, not a target.

| Conviction | % of Account | Dollar Risk | Contracts ($3 spread) | Contracts ($1.30 spread*) |
|------------|-------------|-------------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| LOW (1-3)  | 1.0%        | $100        | 0 -- do not trade    | 1 (risk = $130, round up) |
| MED (4-6)  | 2.0%        | $200        | 1 (risk = $300, accept overshoot with hard stop at $200) | 1-2 (risk = $130-260) |
| HIGH (7-8) | 3.0%        | $300        | 1 (risk = $300) | 2 (risk = $260) |
| MAX (9-10) | 5.0%        | $500        | 1-2 (risk = $300-600, only if multiple confirming catalysts) | 3 (risk = $390) |

*$1.30 spread = Borey's recommended $674/$668 put spread from v2 critique.

### Critical Notes on This Table

1. **LOW conviction means do not trade the $3 spread.** If you are 1-3 on conviction, you either skip the trade entirely or use the narrowest, cheapest spread available. The $1.30 Borey spread is appropriate at low conviction because losing $130 is tuition, not damage.

2. **MED conviction (our current assessment) means 1 contract on either spread.** On the $3 spread, the nominal max loss ($300) exceeds the $200 target. The hard stop at the spread's midpoint ($1.50 on a $3.00 debit) caps realized loss at ~$150. On the $1.30 spread, max loss is already under 2%. One contract. Period.

3. **HIGH conviction allows mild scaling but not doubling.** Going from 1 contract to 2 contracts on the $1.30 spread. The $3 spread stays at 1 contract because you cannot buy fractional spreads and 2 contracts = $600 risk = 6% of account.

4. **MAX conviction is reserved for situations where you have 3+ independent confirming signals firing simultaneously.** Direction + timing + magnitude all at 8+. This happens rarely. If you find yourself at MAX conviction on your first trade week, you are probably wrong -- overconfidence is the most dangerous state for a new trader.

5. **These percentages are PORTFOLIO-LEVEL caps.** If you have two positions open (bear put spread + bear call credit spread), their combined risk must stay within the conviction band. At MED conviction: $200 total across ALL positions. Not $200 per position.

## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

### Before Market Opens Monday

1. Read the CURRENT ASSESSMENT section. Confirm the conviction level still feels right.
2. Check weekend news against the CONVICTION ADJUSTMENTS table. Did anything happen?
3. If conviction has changed, read the new sizing from the table. That is your position size. End of discussion.

### At Entry (Thursday Post-PCE)

1. PCE prints at 8:30 AM. Check the table: was it hot, soft, or neutral?
2. Adjust conviction per the table. Read the new sizing. Execute.
3. If conviction has dropped to LOW (3 or below): skip the trade. There is always next week.
4. If conviction has risen to HIGH (7-8): you may add 1 contract. Not 2. Not 3. One.

### During the Trade

1. A headline fires. Check the table. Adjust conviction. Read the sizing change.
2. If the adjustment says "hold existing position, do NOT add" -- you hold. You do not add.
3. If the adjustment says "close positions" -- you close. That day. Not tomorrow.

### The Meta-Rule

**If you catch yourself arguing with the table, your conviction is emotional, not analytical.** The table was built on Sunday with a clear head. The argument is happening on Thursday with money on the line. Trust Sunday-you over Thursday-you. Sunday-you was thinking. Thursday-you is feeling.
