Also invalid if VIX collapses below 16, which means the market is fully pricing in peace and your put will bleed theta with no catalyst.
- **Edge:**
  1. **Gap fills are statistically reliable.** SPX gaps of >2% fill within 5 trading days roughly 70% of the time. This was a 2.6% gap.
  2. **The ceasefire is weak.** 2-week pause, 3 ships, Iran still blocking. This is a headline, not a resolution. Any Houthi attack, any Iranian statement, any ship turned back will reverse this instantly.
  3. **Macro catalysts incoming.** PCE Thursday and CPI Friday. If inflation is hot, the Fed narrative shifts hawkish and this gap gets sold hard. If inflation is cool, the market may hold — but that is priced in.
  4. **VIX at 20 means puts are not cheap but not expensive.** You are not buying inflated panic premium. You are buying reasonable premium into a binary week.
  5. **Why puts over spreads:** A put spread (e.g., 670/660) would cost ~$2.50 and cap gains at $7.50. The long put has unlimited downside capture. If the ceasefire actually collapses mid-week, SPX could overshoot the gap fill to 6550 and the long put prints $18+. The spread caps you. For a 1-contract position, the extra $2.30 of premium is worth the uncapped tail.

## Risk Notes for Borey

- **Theta is your enemy.** 4 DTE means you lose roughly $0.80-1.00/day if SPY goes nowhere. This trade MUST move by Wednesday or you are bleeding out.
- **Tuesday is the key day.** If SPY does not break below 675 by Tuesday close, strongly consider exiting for whatever the put is worth, even at a loss smaller than the stop.
- **Do NOT hold through Friday expiration.** If you are still in this by Thursday afternoon and the trade has not worked, exit before CPI. Holding a 670P into CPI with 0 DTE is a coin flip, not a trade.
- **Size is conservative.** 1 contract on a $10k account. If you have stronger conviction, 2 contracts at the same stop ($2.80) risks $400 or 4% — aggressive but defensible if you believe the gap fill is >60% likely.
