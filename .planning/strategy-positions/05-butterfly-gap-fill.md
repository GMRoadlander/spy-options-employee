# Position 05: Put Butterfly — Gap Fill to 661

**Thesis:** SPX gapped up from ~6610 to ~6783 on ceasefire news (April 4-6). Gaps of this magnitude in SPX have a historical tendency to fill. If the rally fades and SPX retraces to ~6610 (SPY ~661), a put butterfly centered at that level offers an asymmetric payout: tiny debit risked, massive profit if price pins the target.

- **Action:** Buy 1x SPY 666P / Sell 2x SPY 661P / Buy 1x SPY 656P, April 25 expiry (2026-04-25)

- **Risk/reward ratio:** ~1:7 to 1:10 (risk $1,650 avg to make up to $13,350)

## Why a Put Butterfly

**Cheap lottery ticket with defined risk.** A put butterfly is the ideal structure when you have a specific price target and a reason to believe the underlying will land there:

1. **Tiny max loss.** The entire risk is the debit paid — under $2K on a $10K account. If the gap never fills, or if SPY blows through 661 to 640, the max loss is the same small debit.
2. **Huge payout at the pin.** If SPY settles at or very near 661, each butterfly is worth close to the full $5 wing width. The payout ratio is roughly 7-10:1.
3. **Theta works FOR you near the center.** As expiry approaches, if SPY is near 661, the butterfly's value accelerates toward max profit. You are short the two center puts (which decay fastest) and long the wings.
4. **Volatility is elevated post-gap.** High IV inflates put prices, but a butterfly is vol-neutral — you are long and short similar amounts of vega. The structure is nearly immune to IV crush, unlike a naked put or put spread.
5. **Directional conviction without directional risk.** You profit from mean reversion to a specific level, not from a broad directional move. The gap fill is a precise thesis and the butterfly is a precise instrument.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk Factor | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Gap never fills — rally continues | Max loss = debit (~$1,650) | Acceptable on $10K account; position sized for total loss |

| Liquidity on $5-wide SPY butterflies | Wider bid-ask spread on the butterfly | Use limit orders, leg in if needed; SPY is the most liquid ETF |
| Pin risk at expiry (assignment on short puts) | Auto-exercise if ITM at expiry | Close position before 3:30 PM on expiry day |

## Expiry Choice: April 25 (Not April 18)

**April 25 is the correct expiry.** Here is the reasoning:

1. **Time for the thesis to play out.** The gap just formed (April 4-6). A gap fill is not an overnight event — it typically requires a catalyst reversal, profit-taking, or macro disappointment. 19 calendar days (April 25) gives the thesis room to develop. April 18 (12 days) is too aggressive.

2. **Gamma ramp is still powerful.** At 19 DTE, the butterfly has moderate theta and accelerating gamma as expiry approaches. If SPY drifts toward 661 during the week of April 21-25, the butterfly's value will expand rapidly. April 18 would need the move to happen in a compressed window.

3. **Catalyst calendar supports April 25.** Earnings season ramps up the week of April 14-18, and any negative macro surprise (jobs data, Fed commentary, geopolitical reversal) could trigger the gap fill. April 25 captures the full earnings impact window.

4. **Cost difference is marginal.** The extra week of time value adds ~$0.10-0.15 to the debit. This is trivially small for the additional probability of the thesis playing out.

5. **April 18 would be appropriate only if:** you expected an immediate reversal (Monday/Tuesday) and wanted maximum gamma for a quick fill. But gap fills are probabilistic, not guaranteed, and the extra week is cheap insurance.
