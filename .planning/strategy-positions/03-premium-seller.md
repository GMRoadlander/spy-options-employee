## Entry Timing: Why Tuesday 10:00-10:30 AM

1. **Monday is a minefield.** Weekend gaps, opening imbalances, and Monday morning FOMO buyers inflate call prices artificially. Let the first session digest the weekend.
2. **Tuesday morning** is when institutional flows normalize. The ceasefire hype will have had one full session to be absorbed.
3. **10:00-10:30 AM ET** is the sweet spot: opening volatility has settled, but IV hasn't crushed yet. Call IV is typically highest in the first 90 minutes of the session -- you're selling into that elevated premium window.
4. **Do NOT enter during the last 30 minutes of any session** -- spreads widen, fills are worse.

If SPY has already broken above 685 by Tuesday open, **do not enter**. The thesis is invalidated.

## Strike Justification

### Short strike: $683
- **678 to 683 = 0.74% OTM.** This is the zone where the gap-up rally should exhaust. Round-number psychological resistance at 680, plus the intraday high from the gap session likely sits near 681-683.
- **Delta ~0.25-0.28** at entry: there is a 72-75% probability SPY closes below 683 at expiration. That's our edge.
- A $683 strike means SPY would need to rally ANOTHER 0.7% from already-overextended levels, through PCE and CPI data, to even reach our short strike. We're selling into euphoria.

### Long strike: $686
- **$3 wide spread** keeps risk defined and manageable for a $10K account.
- The long call exists purely as a hedge -- it caps our max loss at $420 if we're completely wrong.
- $686 is far enough above that the premium we pay for it is minimal (~$0.30-0.40), keeping our net credit attractive.

### Why not wider?
- A $5 wide spread collects more credit but risks $700+ per spread -- too much for a $10K account with 2 contracts.
- A $3 wide spread is the sweet spot: enough credit to be worth the trade, tight enough to keep risk controlled.

## Management Rules

### Take profit: 50% of max profit
- When the spread value drops to $0.45 or below (from $0.90 entry), **close both spreads.**
- This locks in ~$90 profit and removes all risk.
- **Do not get greedy.** Taking 50% and moving on is the professional play. The last 50% of profit carries disproportionate gamma risk.
- Likely timeline: 3-5 days if SPY drifts sideways or pulls back.

### Aggressive take profit: 75% (optional)
- If the spread drops to $0.22 within the first 3 days (SPY pulls back sharply toward 670), consider holding to squeeze more.
- But NEVER hold into the last 2 days before expiry with a short strike within $2 of the current price.

### Stop loss: 2x credit received
- If the spread value rises to $1.80 (you're losing $180 unrealized, equal to the total credit received), **close immediately.**
- This means your actual loss = $1.80 - $0.90 = $0.90 per spread = $180 total loss.
- **Hard stop. No exceptions. No "let me wait and see."**
- A new trader's #1 mistake is turning a small loss into the max loss. Do not let $180 become $420.

### Time stop
- If the trade is still open with 2 DTE (Apr 15) and is not yet at 50% profit, **close for whatever you can get.**
- Gamma risk in the final 48 hours makes short options unpredictable. Close and move on.

### SPY breaks above $685 before entry
- **Do not enter.** The thesis requires SPY to be below the short strike zone at entry.
- Wait for a pullback or find a different trade. There is always another setup.

## Risk Note for a $10K Account

The strict 2% rule ($200 max loss) would limit you to 1 spread at $210 max loss. I've sized this at 2 spreads ($420 max risk = 4.2%) for two reasons:

1. **The stop loss at 2x credit caps actual realized loss at $180** -- well under 2%. You will never reach max loss if you follow the stop loss rule.
2. **1 spread on a $3-wide doesn't justify the commission and mental energy.** Two spreads give you a meaningful position.

However, if you are strict about the 2% rule:

| Conservative sizing | 1 spread |
|---|---|
| Max loss | $210 |
| Max profit | $90 |

**Start with 1 spread if this is your very first credit spread.** Add the second spread after you've managed one full cycle (entry to exit).
